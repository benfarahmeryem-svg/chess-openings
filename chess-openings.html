    🔤 The Coordinate System

    The board uses a grid of files (columns a–h, left to right) and ranks (rows 1–8, bottom to top from White's view).

    a b c d e f g h 8 · · · · · · · · 7 · · · · · · · · ... 1 · · · · · · · ·

    e4 = file e, rank 4 (center square). Every square has a unique address.

    🔢 Reading a Game Score

    Moves are numbered. White always goes first. Each turn shows White's move then Black's:

    1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bb5 a6

    Read as: "1. White plays e4, Black plays e5. 2. White plays Knight to f3, Black plays Knight to c6…"

    If only one side's move is shown, use 1…Nf6 (the "…" means Black to move).

    🎯 How to Read "d4"

    A pawn move — no piece letter means it's a pawn. The destination is d4:

    → Find file d (4th column from left)

    → Find rank 4 (4th row from bottom)

    → Move a pawn to that square

    For Nf3: move the Night to f3. Find file f, rank 3.

    🔀 Disambiguation

    When two identical pieces can go to the same square, specify which one:

    Rfd1 — Rook on f-file goes to d1 R1d4 — Rook on rank 1 goes to d4 Nbd2 — Knight on b-file goes to d2

    This only happens with pieces you have two of (both Rooks, both Bishops, both Knights).
